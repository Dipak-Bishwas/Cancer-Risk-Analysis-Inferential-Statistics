# Cancer Risk Analysis & Inferential Statistics

## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing cancer patient healthcare data using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and Inferential Statistics to identify patterns, correlations, and healthcare insights related to cancer severity, treatment costs, survival years, and lifestyle risk factors.

The dataset contains demographic information, environmental factors, lifestyle habits, cancer stages, treatment expenses, and survival-related metrics. The objective of the project is to uncover meaningful statistical relationships and trends that can support healthcare analysis and data-driven decision-making.

---

# Objectives
- Perform data cleaning and preprocessing on healthcare datasets
- Conduct Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Analyze relationships between lifestyle factors and cancer severity
- Identify trends in treatment costs and survival years
- Apply inferential statistical concepts for healthcare insights
- Generate visualizations for better understanding of medical data

---

# Dataset Features
The dataset includes:
- Patient demographics
- Gender and country information
- Genetic risk score
- Air pollution exposure
- Alcohol consumption
- Smoking habits
- Obesity level
- Cancer type and stage
- Treatment cost
- Survival years
- Cancer severity score

# Key Insights
- Higher smoking and obesity levels showed stronger association with cancer severity.
- Advanced cancer stages generally had higher treatment costs.
- Lifestyle and environmental factors influenced survival outcomes.
- Certain cancer types demonstrated significant treatment cost variation.
